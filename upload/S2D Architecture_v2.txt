ROADMAP: S2D FROM DESCRIPTIVE DASHBOARD TO POLITICAL INTELLIGENCE ENGINE

Your target capability should be:

	Apify public-source collection
			↓
	S2D data and evidence foundation
			↓
	Descriptive analysis — what happened?
			↓
	Diagnostic analysis — why did it happen?
			↓
	Predictive analysis — what is likely to happen next?
			↓
	Prescriptive analysis — what should be examined or done?
			↓
	Human-reviewed insight and recommendation reports
			↓
	PIP population-context fusion
			↓
	PIP 360 Intelligence

The most important strategic decision is:

Use PRN Johor as a reference and training corpus, not as a model that is copied directly into Negeri Sembilan and Melaka.

Johor can teach S2D how Malaysian political narratives behave, how issues spread across platforms and how sentiment changes around an election. 
Negeri Sembilan and Melaka will still require their own geography, local vocabulary, candidates, issues, media ecosystems and calibration data.


1. CURRENT S2D MATURITY

Your existing engine is a good descriptive and operational prototype. It already contains:

	Apify-supported social scraping;
	TikTok, Facebook, Instagram and Threads extraction;
	Signal Feed;
	Echo Tracker;
	Narrative Echo;
	locality aggregation;
	sentiment summaries;
	evidence, approval and response workflow;
	outcome recording;
	reporting and export functions.

However, the analytical layer is still mainly heuristic.

For example, the current engine:

	converts the word Negative to a fixed score of 85;
	converts Positive to 20;
	calculates priority using fixed weights;
	hardcodes platform: "TikTok" in the main signal normaliser;
	stores multi-platform signal information inside a tiktok object;
	uses keyword matching for sentiment;
	stores most records locally through window.storage.

Therefore, the current maturity is approximately:

	CAPABILITY				CURRENT MATURITY
	Collection				Functional prototype
	Normalisation			Partial, platform-specific
	Descriptive analysis	Functional
	Diagnostic analysis		Early rules only
	Predictive analysis		Not yet implemented
	Prescriptive analysis	Basic decision rules
	Model validation		Not yet systematic
	Cross-state transfer	Not yet implemented
	PIP integration			Planned through future contracts/API

The architecture you prepared—separating ingestion, monitoring, sentiment, narrative, evidence, response and outcome modules—is the correct direction.

3. THE ANALYTICAL LADDER
LEVEL 1 — DESCRIPTIVE
	Answers:
		What happened?
		How many posts were detected?
		What was the positive/neutral/negative split?
		Which issues and entities were mentioned?
		Which localities generated the most signals?
		What changed compared with yesterday?

	Typical outputs:
		Signal volume
		Net sentiment
		Negative share
		Conversation share
		Engagement
		Narrative velocity
		Source diversity
		Cross-platform spread
		Locality concentration

LEVEL 2 — DIAGNOSTIC
	Answers:
		Why did sentiment change?
		Which issue drove the change?
		Which event triggered the narrative?
		Which sources accelerated it?
		Did the narrative begin locally or nationally?
		Did political pages, community pages or media pages lead the spread?
		Is the apparent increase genuine or caused by duplicate amplification?

LEVEL 3 — PREDICTIVE
	Answers:
		Is this narrative likely to grow within 6, 24 or 72 hours?
		Is it likely to move to the next Echo stage?
		Will it spread into additional localities?
		Is media pickup becoming more likely?
		Is the issue likely to persist?
		What is the expected range of volume and engagement?

LEVEL 4 — PRESCRIPTIVE
	Answers:
		Should the issue be monitored, investigated, clarified or escalated?
		What evidence is missing?
		What response type is appropriate?
		Is responding likely to amplify a low-reach issue?
		Which public-information format is most appropriate?
		What outcome should be monitored after action?

LEVEL 5 — INSIGHT AND RECOMMENDATION
	Produces:
		executive judgement;
		evidence-based interpretation;
		risk assessment;
		scenario outlook;
		recommended actions;
		confidence and limitations;
		locality and constituency implications;
		follow-up monitoring plan.

5. IMPORTANT DISTINCTION: PREDICTIVE POLITICAL SIGNALS VERSUS VOTER FORECASTING
	THE FIRST PREDICTIVE S2D RELEASE SHOULD FORECAST:
		narrative escalation;
		issue persistence;
		engagement;
		cross-platform migration;
		locality spread;
		media pickup;
		response urgency.
	IT SHOULD NOT INITIALLY CLAIM:
		“This party will win.”
		“This voter supports this party.”
		“This demographic will vote for this candidate.”
	ELECTION-OUTCOME FORECASTING REQUIRES A SEPARATE METHODOLOGY USING:
		1. valid surveys;
		2. turnout history;
		3. official electoral data;
		4. candidate effects;
		5. demographic aggregates;
		6. economic indicators;
		7. field observations;
		8. uncertainty modelling.
	SOCIAL SENTIMENT MAY BE ONE FEATURE, BUT IT CANNOT BE TREATED AS VOTER SUPPORT BY ITSELF.

6. RECOMMENDED MODEL ACCEPTANCE GATES
	BEFORE A CAPABILITY IS RELEASED:
		Capability				Minimum acceptance principle
		Sentiment				Beats keyword baseline on human-labelled holdout
		Entity extraction		High precision on party, candidate and agency names
		Issue classification	Stable across language and platform
		Geography				Confidence shown; uncertain records remain unassigned
		Narrative clustering	Analyst review confirms clusters are coherent
		Forecasting				Beats naive forecast in chronological backtesting
		Escalation alerts		False-alert rate is operationally acceptable
		Recommendation			Evidence and explanation always present
		Cross-state model		Recalibrated on state-specific validation data
		Reporting				Observation, inference and recommendation separated
	SUGGESTED INITIAL ENGINEERING TARGETS—NOT GUARANTEES—ARE:
		Sentiment macro-F1:          ≥ 0.75
		Issue classification F1:    ≥ 0.80
		Entity extraction F1:       ≥ 0.85
		High-confidence geography:  ≥ 0.85 precision
		Forecast model:             outperform naive baseline
		Alert calibration:          probabilities reflect actual frequency
